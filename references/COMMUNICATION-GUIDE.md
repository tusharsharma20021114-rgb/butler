# Communication Guide — Butler Reference

## Tone Spectrum

| Level | Style | When to Use |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| **Formal** | No contractions, full titles, structured paragraphs | Legal, executive, external stakeholders |
| **Professional** | Light contractions OK, warm but focused, clear structure | Default for business communication |
| **Casual** | Contractions, friendly, shorter sentences | Team chats, internal updates, familiar contacts |
| **Urgent** | Direct, action-oriented, no pleasantries | Time-critical issues, incidents, deadlines |

## Email Structure Rules

### Opening
- **Formal**: "Dear [Title] [Last Name],"
- **Professional**: "Hi [First Name],"
- **Casual**: "Hey [First Name],"
- **Urgent**: "[First Name] — Action needed by [deadline]:"

### Body Structure
1. **Context** (1-2 sentences): Why you're writing
2. **Core message** (2-4 sentences): What you need to say
3. **Ask/Action** (1-2 sentences): What you need from them
4. **Timeline** (1 sentence): When you need it by

### Closing
- **Formal**: "Sincerely," / "Best regards,"
- **Professional**: "Best," / "Thanks,"
- **Casual**: "Cheers," / "Talk soon,"
- **Urgent**: "Please confirm receipt." / "Awaiting your immediate response."

## Common Scenarios

### Following Up
```
Subject: Following up — [Original Topic]

Hi [Name],

Hope you're well. I wanted to follow up on [topic] from [date/context].

[One sentence restating what was discussed or requested]

Would you have a moment to [specific ask] by [date]?

Thanks,
[Your Name]
```

### Sharing Bad News
```
Subject: Update on [Project/Issue]

Hi [Name],

I want to give you a transparent update on [situation].

[What happened — factual, no blame]

Here's what we're doing about it:
1. [Immediate action]
2. [Follow-up plan]
3. [Prevention measure]

I'll keep you updated on progress. Happy to discuss this further.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Requesting a Meeting
```
Subject: Meeting request — [Topic] (15 min)

Hi [Name],

I'd like to schedule a brief meeting to discuss [topic].

Specifically, I'd like to cover:
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]

Would any of these times work?
- [Option 1]
- [Option 2]
- [Option 3]

Happy to adjust if none work. Thanks!

Best,
[Your Name]
```

### Saying No Professionally
```
Hi [Name],

Thanks for thinking of me for [request].

After careful consideration, I'm not able to take this on right now because [brief, honest reason — capacity, scope, timing].

Here's what I can suggest instead:
- [Alternative 1]
- [Alternative 2]

I hope that helps, and I'm happy to assist with [something you can do] in the future.

Best,
[Your Name]
```

## Message Length Guidelines

| Medium | Target Length | Notes |
|--------|-------------|-------|
| Email | 100-200 words | Scannable, with clear subject line |
| Slack/Teams | 1-3 sentences | Use threads for detail |
| Report | 500-1000 words | Executive summary + details |
| Status update | 3-5 bullet points | What's done, what's next, blockers |

## Tone Mistakes to Avoid

- ❌ **Over-apologizing**: "I'm so sorry to bother you..." → ✅ "I have a quick question."
- ❌ **Passive aggression**: "Per my last email..." → ✅ "To build on our earlier discussion..."
- ❌ **Vague asks**: "Let me know your thoughts" → ✅ "Could you review and approve by Friday?"
- ❌ **Wall of text**: Long paragraphs with no structure → ✅ Use bullet points and bold for key info
